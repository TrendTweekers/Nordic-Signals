## What this is

Nordic Signals is a daily-updating intelligence pipeline for the Nordic tech market. Every morning at 06:00 UTC it scrapes the public career pages of 150+ Nordic startups across Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby, Teamtailor, SmartRecruiters, and Workable, plus Sweden's official JobTech open-data feed. New roles get classified by a small language model into structured signals — AI capability builds, infra investment, leadership hires, "first-ever" roles, fundraise-prep patterns, hardware bets, and so on.

The Monday digest pushes the highest-impact signals to your inbox. Eight items, each with one line of context. Not a job list.

## Why this matters

If you sell to Nordic startups, recruit for them, or invest in them, you already know the signal is hidden in the noise. The "Senior Machine Learning Engineer" posting at a company that previously had zero ML hires is the move. The "Country Head of Merchant Operations" posting at the same company is not. Aggregator job boards don't make that distinction. They show you everything in a sortable table and call it a day.

We make the distinction. A few examples from this week's data, which we pulled before the digest went out:

- **Neko Health** posted a Data Science Lead — their first dedicated data-science leadership hire. The classifier flagged it as `first-ai-capability`. That's the kind of role that precedes a model-driven product expansion, not a Tableau dashboard.
- **Endra** posted a Finance Lead. Early-stage startup, senior finance hire. Tagged `fundraise-prep`. Worth a check-in if you sell tools that startups buy ahead of a raise.
- **ICEYE** posted five avionics and electronics engineers in seven days. Tagged `hardware-bet`. Not a software-only company anymore.
- **Polestar** posted a Head of Cybersecurity. Tagged `compliance-buildout`. Often precedes a regulated-market entry.

These are real rows from the first production run. The full week ran to several hundred relevant roles across the Nordics; the digest surfaces the eight that actually shift a worldview.

## How it works under the hood

Three layers, kept simple on purpose:

1. **Scrape and aggregate.** Direct ATS scraping for the companies we know, plus Sweden's JobTech API for everything we miss. Currently captures roles from 300+ unique Nordic employers.
2. **Filter.** Drop roles in non-Nordic locations, drop non-tech functions (Sales/Marketing/Operations) unless they're strategic leadership hires.
3. **Classify.** A small model tags each surviving row with structured signals. The Monday digest then ranks by signal weight and surfaces the top eight per week.

The whole thing runs free-tier: GitHub Actions cron, Resend free emails, ~€0.20/month of model usage. The point isn't the tech. The point is that nobody else is doing this for the Nordics with this level of curation.
